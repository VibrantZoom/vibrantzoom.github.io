Who Is This Website For?

Animals

Animals, while they may not be whipping up complex chemical equations, inherently use chemistry too, like when a skunk deploys its smelly defense mechanism or when a firefly lights up the night with its bio-luminescent behind. It's all a part of nature's wild, wacky, and wonderfully aromatic chemistry lab!

Humans

Humans use chemistry not only to invent life-changing medications and eco-friendly fuels, but also for equally crucial tasks like perfecting the ultimate chocolate chip cookie recipe. After all, what's the point of understanding molecular structures if you can't bake a cookie that makes your taste buds do a happy dance?

Aliens

Aliens might be using chemistry to perfect their intergalactic barbecue sauce or even brew some out-of-this-world space beer. After all, extraterrestrial or not, who wouldn't want a stellar cookout or a cosmic happy hour?

What Are Chemical Compounds?

Chemical compounds are substances made up of two or more elements, which are chemically bonded together. These bonds can be ionic, where one element loses an electron and the other gains an electron, or covalent, where both elements share electrons. Chemical compounds can be found in everything from organic materials like DNA and proteins to inorganic materials like salts and minerals. In fact, the world is made up of countless types of chemical compounds that play a crucial role in maintaining life and the environment.
